Post-Earthquake Rescue Operation

Required Resources for Rescue Operation



  • Rapid Damage Assement:
    • Rapid Damage Assessment takes place within hours of an incident providing a quick evaluation of a disaster/emergency impacted area.
    • Providing local government with accurate information for an adequate response to life-threatening situations;
    • Determinning the need for additional resources;
    • Assisting with determining local resource allocations;
    • Determining need for local disaster/emergency declaration;
    • Assisting with decision making and actions inthe protection of life and property.
  • Search and Rescue:
    • Urban search and rescue (US&R) involves the location, rescue (extrication), and initial medical stabilization of individuals trapped in confined spaces.
    • Urban search and rescue is considered a "multi-hazard" discipline
    • An immediate life-saving response is required to rescue those who are trapped and stabilise or evacuate survivors.
    • The difference between life and death can be a matter of hours.
  • Emergency Medical Care:
    • Emergency preparedness can be defined as preimpact activities that establish a state of readiness to respond to extreme events that could affect the community.
    • Communicate with local Emergency Operations Center and officials to determine extent damage to critical infrastructure and services
    • Communicate with other hospitals to determine status
    • Assess facility for damage, initiate repairs as appropriate or secure unsafe areas.
    • Activate alternate care sites for evacuated patients, if necessary.
  • Emergency restoration of essential services:
    • Restoration of services immidiately following a disaster is essential to providing adequate rescue efforts and medical services at local facilities.
    • Having power uninterrupted to all medical facilities is crucial to attending to victims.
    • Communication among various agencies and rescue teams is essential for a well-coordinated rescue effort.
  • Crisis decision-making:
    • During times of crisis and disaster, it's important to have a well-defined chain of command in the decision-making process, to ensure everyone is on the same page.
    • Miscommunication among various agencies can mean the difference between life and death for victims of a disaster.
    • The more coordinated an operation is executed, the better chance there is for minimizing casualties.
  • Evacuation, protection of lives and property:
    • Having a proper evacuation plan in place, ensures that those in effected areas are moved to a safe location during the rescue process.
    • Focusing on those who actually need to be rescued or located is the most important job for those searching for them.
    • Those who do not need this type of attention should not be in the area whenre they can impede a rescue operation.
